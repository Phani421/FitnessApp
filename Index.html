Your 12-week roadmap — tailored for age 45. The plan is absolutely achievable. At 45 the key differences are: recovery takes 1 extra day, warm-ups are non-negotiable, and consistency over 12 weeks beats intensity in week 1. You will get there.
⚡ Age 45 — What changes & what doesn't
✅ Nothing changes
• The workout split (Push/Pull/Legs)
• Calorie target (1,800 kcal)
• Protein goal (135g/day)
• Swimming, badminton, cycling
• 12-week timeline is realistic
• 24–28 kg loss is fully achievable
⚠️ Adjust these
• Warm-up: 12–15 min (not 10)
• Rest between sets: 90s (not 60s)
• No 16:8 fasting — eat breakfast
• Skip burpees — replace with step-ups
• Add joint supplements (see below)
• Sleep 8 hrs — non-negotiable at 45
Phase 1 — Foundation (Weeks 1–4)
Goal: Build the habit. Learn the movements. Let joints adapt. Target: lose 3–4 kg. At 45 — form is everything. Do NOT rush weight progression.

Supplements — Age 45 Specific
SupplementDoseWhy it matters at 45
✅ Whey Protein1 scoop post-workoutMuscle protein synthesis slows with age — protein timing matters more
✅ Vitamin D3 + K22,000–4,000 IU D3 dailyCritical for testosterone, bone density, and immunity — most Indians are deficient
✅ Vitamin B12500 mcg dailyEssential for vegetarians — energy, nerve function, red blood cells
✅ Magnesium Glycinate300–400 mg at nightImproves sleep quality, reduces muscle cramps, aids recovery — very underrated at 45
✅ Omega-3 (Algae-based)1g DHA+EPA dailyJoint lubrication, anti-inflammatory, heart health — important for heavy training
✅ Creatine Monohydrate5g daily (from Week 5)Clinically proven to help muscle gain at any age — especially effective after 40
⚡ Zinc25–30 mg dailySupports testosterone levels which decline ~1% per year after 40
Consult your doctor before starting supplements, especially if you have any existing conditions.

RuleWhy
✅ 135–140g protein dailyMuscle preservation is harder at 45 — protein is your #1 tool
